Origin of Hydrogen

Hydrogen was created during the Big Bang nucleosynthesis, approximately 13.8 billion years ago. It's the primordial element from which all other elements are formed through stellar processes.

Where is Hydrogen Found?

Hydrogen makes up about 75% of the universe's baryonic mass. On Earth, it's primarily found in water (H‚ÇÇO) and organic compounds.

  • Universe: 75% by mass
  • Sun: Primary component
  • Earth: In water, organic compounds

Hydrogen in Daily Life

We encounter hydrogen every day, often without realizing it.

  • Water (H‚ÇÇO) - Essential for life
  • Fuels - Gasoline, natural gas
  • Cleaning products - Ammonia-based cleaners
  • Food - Part of carbohydrates, proteins, fats

Hydrogen in the Human Body

Hydrogen is the third most abundant element in the human body by mass (10%) and the most abundant by number of atoms (60%).

  • Water content: ~60% of body weight
  • DNA structure: Hydrogen bonds
  • Proteins: Part of amino acids
  • Energy production: ATP synthesis
