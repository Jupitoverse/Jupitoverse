The Evolution of ISRO: A Timeline

The First Step: Aryabhata

1975

India's journey into space began with the launch of its first satellite, Aryabhata. This momentous event marked the dawn of a new era of scientific exploration for the nation.

Self-Reliance: SLV-3 & Rohini

1980

A major milestone in self-reliance, ISRO successfully launched the Rohini satellite aboard its own launch vehicle, SLV-3, from Indian soil for the first time.

National Impact: INSAT & IRS Era

1990s

ISRO revolutionized daily life in India with its INSAT (telecom, broadcasting) and IRS (remote sensing) satellite systems, becoming the backbone of national infrastructure.

The Workhorse: PSLV's Dominance

1994 - Today

The Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) became ISRO's most trusted rocket. Renowned for its reliability, it has launched hundreds of national and international satellites.

Destination Moon: Chandrayaan-1

2008

India joined the elite club of lunar explorers with Chandrayaan-1. The mission's most significant contribution was the definitive discovery of water molecules on the Moon.

The Mars Miracle: Mangalyaan

2013

In a historic feat, India became the first nation to reach Mars on its maiden attempt with the Mars Orbiter Mission (Mangalyaan), showcasing its mastery of frugal engineering.

Heavy Lift Power: The LVM3 Rocket

2017

With the first successful flight of the LVM3 (GSLV MkIII), ISRO gained the capability to launch its heaviest satellites and the foundation for the Gaganyaan human spaceflight mission.

Touchdown on the South Pole: Chandrayaan-3

2023

A moment of immense national pride. ISRO achieved a flawless soft landing on the lunar south pole, making India the first and only country in the world to do so.

The Future is Now: Aditya-L1 & Gaganyaan

Present & Beyond

The journey continues with Aditya-L1 studying the Sun, Gaganyaan preparing to send Indians into space, and many more ambitious missions that will continue to push the boundaries of science.
